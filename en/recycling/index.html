What is Recycling?

Recycling is the process of using waste materials to create new products. Instead of burying our waste in landfills which threaten the environment, we can return it to the resource pool, thereby reducing the extraction and consumption of new raw materials from the earth. Recycling reduces energy usage, air pollution and water pollution, which in turn offers us all healthier lives. By responsibly separating our waste, we have a positive impact by protecting the environment, conserving energy, and keeping Georgia beautiful.

As the economy grows, more products are consumed and more waste is created, which ultimately is buried or burned in landfills. Burning waste diminishes the health of the millions of people who breathe polluted air. Buried waste leaks into underground water systems, contaminating the water which ultimately is used on the crops we eat, in the water supplies where we drink, and in the lakes where we swim.

Benefits of Recycling:

  • reduces the waste which goes into landfills
  • conserves natural resources such as timber, water, and minerals
  • prevents pollution
  • saves energy
  • reduces public expenditure (your tax money)
  • helps sustain the environment for your future generations

Facts about Recycling:

  • Up to 60% of household trash can be recycled
  • Creating plastics from recycled material saves 88% of the energy required to make new plastics from gas and oil
  • Plastic takes up to 500 years to biodegrade
  • Recycling 1 ton of paper saves 17 trees, 25,000 liters of water, 3 cubic meters of landfill space, 2 barrels of oil, and 4,000 kWh of electricity
  • Recycled paper produces 73% less air pollution than if it was made from raw materials
  • A glass bottle will never decompose
  • Glass is 100% recyclable and can used again and again
  • Over a ton of natural resources are conserved for every ton of glass that is recycled
