What is Recycling?

Recycling is the process of using waste materials to create new products. Instead of burying our waste in landfills which threaten the environment, we can return it to the resource pool, thereby reducing the extraction and consumption of new raw materials from the earth. Recycling reduces energy usage, air pollution and water pollution, which in turn offers us all healthier lives. By responsibly separating our waste, we have a positive impact by protecting the environment, conserving energy, and keeping Georgia beautiful.

As the economy grows, more products are consumed and more waste is created, which ultimately is buried or burned in landfills. Burning waste diminishes the health of the millions of people who breathe polluted air.

The Green Office Campaign

Every day all of us make decisions that affect global warming, air pollution, and other environmental issues.

Everyday decisions in the workplace are factors that can affect our environment in negative ways. To list just a few:

Have we ever considered whether we really need to overheat the office? Maybe we could fix our heaters two degrees lower and wear an extra sweater, instead of wearing t-shirts at the expense of the resources that extra heating takes. After all, why not enjoy all four seasons and let their diversity reflect in our outfits?

Have we ever considered where the waste accumulated in our offices goes? The waste in our offices is a huge contributor to various environmental problems. But it is also a factor whose impact can be alleviated through conscious minimizing of office consumption on the one hand, and making an effort to recycle our waste, on the other.

Do any of us ever consider whether we really must go to the other end of the city to settle a minor issue? Maybe a phone call or an e-mail message would do? And do we really need to drive separately to our workplaces? Maybe we could share rides or simply walk or bike to the offices. Any means for minimizing our office-related transportation not only saves money but also contributes to saving our planet.

The phrase "Green Office" refers to the mentality and practices that help reduce the environmental impact of our office activities and make a real difference.

CENN and Coop Georgia are conducting a campaign to involve more offices in green practices. The Green Office Campaign is about encouraging offices to separate waste, as well as helping them to get rid of the separated waste through Coop Georgia's regular recycling pick-up service. How does our service work?

  1. Our bins are placed with restaurants, businesses, communities, and in public locations
  2. We pick up the recycling reguarly
  3. It goes to our sorting facility
  4. The materials are sorted, compacted, and stored
  5. Last, we take it to our partner factories - glass is used to make new bottles, paper is used to make toilet paper, and plastic is refabricated into new plastics
